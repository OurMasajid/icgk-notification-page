Sunnah of Eid al-Adha & the Day of Arafah

  • Fasting on the Day of Arafah (Tuesday, May 26th) is highly recommended for those not performing Hajj. The Prophet ﷺ said it expiates the sins of the previous year and the coming year.
  • Make abundant duʻa on the Day of Arafah — it is among the best days of the year for supplication. Remember your family, the Ummah, and those in need.
  • Increase in Takbir, Tahmid, and Tahlil from the dawn of Arafah through the days of Tashreeq.
  • Perform Udhiya (Qurbani / Sacrifice) after the Eid prayer if you are able. It is a confirmed Sunnah commemorating the sacrifice of Prophet Ibrahim ﷺ.
  • Recommended Sunnahs on Eid day: take a Ghusl, wear your best clothes, apply perfume (for men), and do not eat before the Eid prayer (eat from your Udhiya afterward).
  • Give generously — share meat from your Udhiya with family, neighbors, and the poor.
